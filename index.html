The Paper Clip Method.
Proven for 30 years.

In 1993, a 23-year-old stockbroker named Trent Dyrsmid started each morning with two jars and 120 paper clips. Every completed sales call - one clip moved across. Within 18 months, he was generating $5 million a year for his firm.

A cue you can't miss
Most habits fail because we forget to start. A jar of clips on your desk - or your phone - is a cue that's always there. ClipIt keeps that cue with you wherever you go.
Progress that compounds
Each clip you move makes the next one easier. Behavioural economists call this the Endowed Progress Effect - the more you've done, the more motivated you become to finish.
A reward that feels earned
Dragging a clip into the done jar and hearing it land isn't just good UX. It's the feedback loop that makes habits stick - and the reason the Paper Clip Method has lasted 30 years.

How ClipIt works.

No onboarding course. No 47-step system. Three steps.

01

Add your tasks as clips

Create each task as a paper clip. Colour it by effort - green for the easy ones, blue for the work that needs focus, red for the tasks you've been putting off.

02

Work through your day

Your jar shows you exactly what's left. No infinite list - just a clear, honest view of your day. Work through it one clip at a time.

03

Drag it across. Feel it land.

Complete a task, drag the clip to the done jar, and hear it clink. Watch your progress build. It's more satisfying than crossing something off a list - and that's the point.
