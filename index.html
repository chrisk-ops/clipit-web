The Paper Clip Method.
30 years of real results.

In 1993, a 23-year-old stockbroker named Trent Dyrsmid started each morning with two jars and 120 paper clips. Every completed sales call, one clip moved across. Within 18 months, he was generating $5 million a year for his firm.

A cue you can't miss
Most habits fail because we forget to start. A jar of paperclips on your desk - or your phone - is a cue that's always there. ClipIt keeps that cue with you wherever you go.
Progress that compounds
Each clip you move makes the next one easier. Behavioural economists call this the Endowed Progress Effect - the more you've done, the more motivated you become to finish.
A reward that feels earned
There's something genuinely satisfying about dragging a clip across and hearing it drop. It's a small reward - but small rewards, done consistently, are what turn intentions into habits.

How ClipIt works.

Two jars. One simple rule. Move the clip when the task is done.

01

Fill your jar

Drop your tasks in as clips at the start of the day. You can see exactly what you're taking on - nothing forgotten at the bottom of a list.

02

Do the work

Pick a task and do it. ClipIt sits quietly showing you what's left without nagging you about it. Work through your jar at your own pace.

03

Move your tasks across

Finish a task, drag the clip across, and hear it drop into the right jar. At the end of the day you can see exactly what you got done, right there in front of you.
